📰 IEEE Internet of Things Journal · Published
FoCLAP-Net: Interpretable Multimodal Deep Learning for Wearable Sensor Posture Recognition

Designed in collaboration with Université du Québec à Chicoutimi (UQAC), Canada. Novel multimodal architecture combining focal attention with cross-modal learned attention pooling for wearable sensor time-series. Achieves 98.66% classification accuracy with real-time inference on rehabilitation posture data. Improves clinical interpretability through modality-aware attention mechanisms that highlight which sensor modalities drive each classification decision.

98.66% accuracy · Real-time inference · Clinical interpretability

FAN-based Deep Learning for Communication Channel Feedback Prediction

Developed in collaboration with Nokia Research. Novel application of Fourier Analysis Networks (FAN) with attention mechanisms and spatial feature modeling to the problem of feedback prediction in wireless communication channels. Surpasses existing state-of-the-art by a 90% performance margin, demonstrating the power of frequency-domain representations for signal modeling tasks in next-generation networks. Accepted and to be presented at IEEE ICC 2026 in Glasgow, UK.

+90% over SotA · IEEE ICC 2026 · Nokia collaboration

Hybrid KAN Architecture for Robust Deepfake Detection

Proposed a novel hybrid Kolmogorov–Arnold Networks architecture combining learnable activation functions with deep feature extraction pipelines for robust deepfake detection. Achieved 98.7% AUC, a +6.6% improvement over prior state-of-the-art on standard benchmarks. Demonstrated superior generalization across deepfake generation methods not seen during training.

98.7% AUC · +6.6% vs SotA · CNRS/LIRIS
